About FacultyGuard

Built from the faculty side of the desk.

FacultyGuard was founded by Prof. M. Ahad Hayaud-Din, a political scientist, faculty leader, former department chair, online educator, and public scholar with more than twenty-five years in higher education.

The problem

The modern faculty role has absorbed LMS management, AI policy enforcement, academic integrity documentation, dispute response, record-keeping, rubric maintenance, and multi-section consistency. Most graduate programs and institutional orientations do not prepare faculty to manage that operational burden.

The mission

FacultyGuard builds the professional infrastructure that allows faculty to exercise judgment with confidence, protect standards with clarity, and teach without apology.

The philosophy

Professional sovereignty means that a faculty member’s decisions, standards, and time remain genuinely theirs to control, defend, and sustain. FacultyGuard does not replace judgment. It builds the infrastructure that allows judgment to hold.

The founder

Prof. Din’s work draws on more than twenty-five years of teaching, curriculum design, academic leadership, distance education, faculty advocacy, assessment, accreditation, and instructional technology. FacultyGuard translates that lived experience into usable systems for other faculty.

The voice of FacultyGuard is the voice of a senior colleague who has solved these problems in practice and is willing to share the system.
