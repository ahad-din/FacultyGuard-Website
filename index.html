Why FacultyGuard Exists

Teaching changed. Faculty were left to build the systems alone.

Across higher education, educators repeatedly solve the same operational problems in isolation: grading volume, student communication, documentation, academic integrity, AI policy, and semester closeout. FacultyGuard documents what works, organizes it into reusable systems, and returns that knowledge to the profession.

You do not have to carry the operational burden of teaching in isolation.

Questions From Faculty

FacultyGuard FAQ

Is FacultyGuard an AI grading tool?

No. FacultyGuard does not grade student work or replace instructor judgment. AI may be used as one implementation tool, but the product is the professional method, workflow, and faculty-controlled system.

Who is FacultyGuard designed for?

Higher education instructors, adjuncts, online and hybrid faculty, department coordinators, faculty leaders, and academic programs managing complex teaching workflows.

Can FacultyGuard work with Canvas, D2L, Blackboard, or Moodle?

Yes. FacultyGuard is LMS-neutral. Systems can be adapted to the course platform and institutional environment already in use.

Can I receive ongoing support after implementation?

Yes. Monthly support plans provide continued review, refinement, implementation guidance, and coverage for evolving semester needs.

Does FacultyGuard support departments and institutions?

Yes. Custom partnership engagements can support multi-section coordination, shared workflows, program implementation, faculty development, and institutional documentation.
